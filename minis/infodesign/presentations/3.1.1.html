Communicating Visually.


Writing.


Culturally ingrained communication tool, originally for religious and business purposes.

Drawing.


Communicates the human condition and the experiences during a given period.

Symbolism.


Representative symbolism and iconography established through cultural practices.

Notation.


Tally Marks.


Combining multiple individual items into a collection in order to assess them as a whole.

Numerals.


More efficient representations of quantities using fewer glyphs.

Music Notation.


Attempting to communicate the attributes of something non-visual in a visual form.

The Visual Cortex.


Analysis of Graphic Elements.


Visual analysis of graphic elements before you have to consciously think about it.

Immediately Recognize Patterns.


Patterns and trends reveal themselves when viewing a holistic representation of activity.

Emergent Qualities.


Individual graphic elements working in conjunction to display more about the subject.

Retinal Variables.


Visual Properties.


Size, value, texture, color, orientation, shape.

Dimensionality.


Association, selection, order, quantity.

Level of Variation.


Amount of variation of a given retinal variable determines the level of detail.

Visual Identity.


Consistency Across Graphic Elements.


Ensuring the visual language that is established is consistently applied.

Clarity and Functionality Through Form.


Using the formal choices as functional elements of the design.

Legibility and Reverse Readability.


Priority on accessibility to viewers as well as a natural reverse readability of the underlying data.
