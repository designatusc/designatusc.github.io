How to most effectively use the reference.


Chronological.


Organize information design projects based on their date of creation in order to reflect on the historical conditions during their production.

Visual Forms.


Analyze the visual organizational forms used for communicating the different subjects and how they effectively tell the viewer a story.

Rendering Methods.


Group representations based on the means and technologies that were used to create them.

Classic Infographics.


Traditional Rendering Methods.


Sketching, illustration, drafting, woodcutting, lithography, process printing.

Pre-computational Analysis.


Data needed to be acquired, managed, and analyzed on paper requiring much of the mental lifting to be done by humans.

Limited Complexity.


Difficulties in data acquisition and analysis meant that the level of complexity of the subject matter being analyzed was limited.

Cartography.


Spatial Representation.


Attempting to create an abstraction of our real-world experience.

Utility for Wayfinding.


Maps are tools used for getting from A to B and become situationally specific in the context in which people use them.

Importance of Scale.


Resolution of details in the topography are determined by the level of scale being represented by the map.

Contemporary Infographics.


Rendered on Computer.


Custom software is used to render visual representations of data in raster or vector format to be used for display on screen or print.

Data Software Management.


Databases and spreadsheets can be used as management infrastructure to help handle the large amounts of data for analysis and rendering.

Capacity for Interaction.


The ability to render data to screen repeatedly allows the functionality of interacting with the data representation through a user interface.
