Understanding Your Data.


Epistomology.


Understanding and identifying the differences between data, information, knowledge, and wisdom and how these are acquired.

Data Qualities.


Data is ubiquitous, diverse, and important to understand in order to make more informed decisions about the future.

Evaluating Data.


Data must be assessed for its component properties and how those components can be used to make comparisons which can be presented as particular perspectives into the subject matter.

Analog Rendering Methods.


Sketching.


The initial visual explorations of the potential forms that a data set can take in order to communicate a perspective to the intended audiences.

Drafting.


A more precise way of illustrating the graphic elements required to effectively communicate the underlying subject matter.

Printing.


A means of reproducing visual designs by transfering the imagery to a printing process such as lithography or engraving.

Digital Rendering Methods.


Illustration Software.


Digital illustration software affords the designer many tools intended to simulate the effects of traditional illustrative tools, offering greater control and a more intuitive approach.

Statistical Analysis Software.


Data management software provides a number of utilities for running analysis and exporting simple visualizations of underlying data.

Data Visualization Software.


Data visualization software specifically intended to produce elegant visual results of information, often alleviates much of the design work.
