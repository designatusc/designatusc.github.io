What is Hypermedia?


Multimedia.


Multimedia is a mixture of varying types of communicative mediums ranging from text to illustration, audio to video.

Nonlinearity.


Nonlinearity deals with the way a user consumes content over a period of time and in what order that content is consumed.

Navigation.


Hypermedia enables users to navigate across a variety of media related to a specific subject matter using hyperlinks.

Nonlinearity.


Links Between Content.


Hyperlinks are the connective tissue tying hypermedia content together, allowing users to jump between media types and tangentially related subjects.

Associations Across Media.


Hyperlinks can be created to tie together different types of media that share a common subject matter.

Order of Consumption.


As the user has the freedom to navigate across the content in a nonlinear fashion, they get to choose the order in which they consume it.

Navigating Content.


User Navigation Choices.


The user's ability to navigate through the content on their own achord affords them the ability to dive into or ignore particular bits of material.

Breadth vs. Depth.


Navigating through hypermedia can provide the user with the ability to go in depth in their subject matter or acquire a wider breadth of knowledge related to it.

Media Preference.


Since hypermedia allows for a variety of different media formats, the user can select to peruse the format they find most effective for learning about the subject.
