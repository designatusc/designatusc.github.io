Reactive Elements.


Action/Reaction.


A visual response to user activity ensures that the user is aware of the effect they are having on the interface and underlying system.

Input/Response.


When a system receives new input, that change is reflected in the interface in order to provide an informative response to the user.

Latency of Experience.


The lower the latency of the interface experience, the more closely connected the user feels with the underlying system.

Motion Graphics.


Aesthetics of Motion.


The way graphic elements change their visual properties over time determines a specific aesthetic quality for the interface.

Event Response.


Reacting to events and transitioning between different states of behavior helps communicate the currently functioning state of the system.

Visual Language.


Animations and transitions of visual elements are a visual language of their own that the interface designer must determine to most effective communicate to the user.

Science Fiction Interface Design.


Speculative Interfaces.


Creators of science fiction have had the freedom to explore different approaches to interface without being concerned with how they are engineered.

Fictional Functionality.


Many science fiction interfaces are designed around a fictional functionality, often as a component of the storyline or the system that the interface is a part of.

Advancements in Technology.


Science fiction usually projects a great deal of technological advancement which accommodates a more unconventional and innovative interface experience.
